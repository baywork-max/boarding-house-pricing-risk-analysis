# boarding-house-pricing-risk-analysis

## Background
A boarding house business plans to expand by developing new rooms in a different location.
Existing rooms show mixed payment behaviors, including on-time payment, late payment,
and failed payment based on montly payment. Management requires data-driven pricing guidance to reduce payment
risk while maintaining revenue potential.

## Business Problem
What pricing strategy should be applied to new boarding house rooms to balance
revenue generation and payment reliability?

## Objective
- Analyze payment behavior across different room prices
- Measure revenue potential adjusted by payment risk
- Recommend an optimal pricing strategy for expansion at another land

## Data Overview
The dataset consists of 10 boarding rooms with:
- Room type
- Monthly price
- Payment status(every 25 day)

## Tools Used
- Excel / Google Sheets
- Descriptive analysis
- Risk-adjusted revenue calculation
- Business reasoning

## Key Insights (Summary)
- Lower-priced rooms show more stable payment behavior
- Higher-priced rooms generate higher potential revenue but carry higher payment risk
- Risk-adjusted revenue provides a more realistic pricing perspective

## Final Recommendation (Summary)
- Maintain IDR 800,000 for non-AC rooms
- Adjust AC room pricing for new locations to reduce default risk
- Introduce risk mitigation mechanisms for higher-priced rooms
